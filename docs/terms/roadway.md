# roadway

part of the surface of the [road reservation](road reservation.md) primarily designed for the movement of [vehicles](vehicle.md) that conform to a specified set of requirements

</object>

Clause: 3.3.1.3

Note 1 to entry: A roadway is characterized using a [cross-section](cross-section.md) of a road facility as the roadway characteristics often change along the length of the road. See [road](road.md), [road section](road section.md), [road segment](road segment.md), and [road link](road link.md) for different types of stretches of roadway.

Note 2 to entry: In practice, the roadway design considers a variety of issues, which can result in suboptimal [road segments](road segment.md) that violate normal design guidelines. Such [locations](spatial location.md) are typically posted with warning signs, but they are still designed for the movement of vehicles.

Note 3 to entry: The roadway includes any contiguous [hard shoulders](hard shoulder.md), [hardstanding](hardstanding.md) and [reserved lanes](reserved lane.md).

Note 4 to entry: While the roadway is designed with certain vehicle characteristics in mind, local regulations typically allow a wider set of vehicles to use the roadway.

History note: Introduced in ISO/TS 14812:2022
