# dynamic route

<transport service> [concurrent](concurrent.md) operation where transported items can only be received or delivered at stopping points within a pre-defined [service](service.md) corridor

<object type="image/svg+xml" data="../../diagrams/dynamic route.dot.svg">
    <img alt="dynamic route Diagram" src="../../diagrams/dynamic route.dot.png" /> <!-- Fallback for non-SVG browsers -->
</object>

Clause: 3.5.9.5

EXAMPLE: Airport shuttle.

Note 1 to entry: The service corridor is defined by the provider, who may impose further restrictions on where stops are allowed.

Note 2 to entry: The service corridor often includes a communal point of interest (e.g. an airport, transit hub, etc.) as a fixed stopping point.

History note: Introduced in ISO/TS 14812:2022
