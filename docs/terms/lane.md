# lane

<generic> portion of [road reservation](road reservation.md) intended to accommodate a single line of moving [material entities](material entity.md) along its length

<object type="image/svg+xml" data="../../diagrams/lane.dot.svg">
    <img alt="lane Diagram" src="../../diagrams/lane.dot.png" /> <!-- Fallback for non-SVG browsers -->
</object>

Clause: 3.3.1.12

Alternative preferred term: generic lane

EXAMPLE: [Traffic lane](traffic lane.md), [cycle lane](cycle lane.md), [sidewalk](sidewalk.md).

Note 1 to entry: [Lanes](lane.md) are often bounded by lane markings.

Note 2 to entry: Lanes may be significantly wider than the normal [vehicle](vehicle.md) width to allow variance in lane position of the vehicle as well as for various vehicle dimensions.

Note 3 to entry: The "lane" nomenclature typically refers to [motor vehicle](motor vehicle.md) usage, but can also refer to other purposes, such as [sidewalks](sidewalk.md).

History note: Introduced in ISO/TS 14812:2022
